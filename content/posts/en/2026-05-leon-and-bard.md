[After 51 years as Bard College's President, Leon Botstein announced his resignation last Friday.](https://michaeldeibert.substack.com/p/finally-on-leon?utm_id=97757_v0_s00_e232_tv2_tp1_a1dennhb5exu32&fbclid=IwY2xjawRnvPhleHRuA2FlbQIxMQBzcnRjBmFwcF9pZBAyMjIwMzkxNzg4MjAwODkyAAEe9Fvn08IyPZjESb6MNw-7XiHznKDTJbEOEEXef_UtOSWQgomVpmLJU8GLPWE_aem_LDIROecu4I-BG-ncZN4pEw)  This was days after the release of the [WilmerHale report](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1axoPl_FPhABe3hY8_xGminVeNgh0gnan/view?fbclid=IwY2xjawRnvRNleHRuA2FlbQIxMQBzcnRjBmFwcF9pZBAyMjIwMzkxNzg4MjAwODkyAAEenO8tmxFzWPI_zJpy5gjQ4Sw7_K-KUNJ_HukW5gg0c98aks9ZDBFFUJQZJGQ_aem_cNlWgbuUm9cR7rIoR53vJg) on his relationship with notable sex offender Jeffrey Epstein, who Botstein had courted for donations relentlessly, even after Epstein's conviction.

My own relationship with Bard is now very distant; I attended Bard from 1989 to 1991 and participated in its first exchange program with St Peterburg's Herzen Institute, establishing a foothold for Bard and its relationship with the Smolny instituate years later.  I also wrote for [the Bard Observer](https://digitalcommons.bard.edu/observer/) during that time, and interviewed Botstein at least once, probably several times.  After 1991, I only returned to its campus once, to see some of my friends graduate in 1993.

I'm not surprised that Botstein pursued Epstein for money.  I'm not surprised that he ignored caution and brushed off the warnings of senior faculty.  I **am** surprised that he finally gave in and resigned.
