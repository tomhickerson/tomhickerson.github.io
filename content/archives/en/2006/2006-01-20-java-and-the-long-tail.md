title: "Java and the long tail"
date: 2006-01-20
---

<P>The previous two posts were a preface to this one. I'm not kidding. For a little while I have been reading about a post on the <a href="http://longtail.typepad.com/the\_long\_tail/2005/03/the\_long\_tail\_o.html">Long Tail of Software</a>, which was based off of a blog, which was in turn based off of Chris Anderson's <a href="http://www.wired.com/wired/archive/12.10/tail.html">Wired article</a>. <a href="http://longtail.typepad.com/the\_long\_tail/2005/03/the\_long\_tail\_o.html"><img src="http://www.jroller.com/resources/w/wj/excite.gif" title="Excite" alt="Excite" style="margin: 0px 0px 5px 5px; float: right;"/></a> <P>This post, in turn, points to another article by the founder of Jotspot, who stands behind the philosophy that we can begin to serve millions of markets of dozens, rather than dozens of markets of millions. The idea to get software to the place where it is so customizable that businesses can use it to shape their own highly unique processes struck a chord with me because it is mirroring what is going on in the Java world today. <P>In an <a href="http://jroller.com/page/wj?entry=too\_many\_cooks">earlier post</a>, I did a little grousing about the fact that we have too much choice in the Java framework world. Should we get behind Struts or Spring? Tapestry or eXo? Velocity or Slingshot? Drools or ...? You get the idea. Sun certainly would love for us all to become <b>EJB</b> supporters and embrace one standard, but that will never happen. <P>The truth is, every in-house developer will find that one framework will hit the spot on a certain problem, whereas another framework will do the job better for another job. The framework over-choice, then, is Java EE's own long tail. As long as there are developers who need to get a certain job done quick, there will be a long list of frameworks, each with their own strengths and weaknesses. The problem now will be how does one developer start to specialize. Do we become 'the framework guy' or the 'object-relational mapping guy'? <P>PS -- <i>I do have to give some more credit where credit is due: I first heard about the combination of the <a href="http://www.dulance.com/long-tail.html">long tail and the web</a> through the shopping search engine <a href="http://www.dulance.com/about.html">Dulance</a>, which isn't listed yet on <a href="http://battellemedia.com/">Battele's blog</a>, but should be</i>.
